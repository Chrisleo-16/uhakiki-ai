🇰🇪 The Vision
In an era of sophisticated deepfakes and document tampering, UHAKIKI AI (Swahili for Verification) serves as the digital shield for Kenya's national identity infrastructure. We move beyond simple OCR by using Neural Residual Analysis to detect pixel-level anomalies that the human eye—and standard scanners—miss.

Key Innovation: The Neural OODA Loop
UHAKIKI AI operates on an Agentic OODA Loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act), ensuring that every identity ingest is not just recorded, but audited in real-time.

Observe: Multi-spectral image ingestion with Laplacian Variance quality filters.
Orient: Conversion of documents into Neural Embeddings via Autoencoder bottlenecks.
Decide: Residual Heatmap generation—calculating the Mean Squared Error (MSE) between the "Expected" and "Actual" document structure.
Act: Automatic vaulting into Milvus for biometric deduplication or flagging for the Agentic Review Council.

🤝 Contribution & Governance

⚖️ Data Governance & DPA 2019 Compliance
UHAKIKI AI is architected to exceed the statutory requirements of the Kenya Data Protection Act, No. 24 of 2019. Our engine implements "Privacy by Design" (Section 41) as a core operational requirement.

1. Data Protection Principles (Section 25)

- Our Neural Engine strictly adheres to the eight pillars of the DPA:

  (i) Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency: Users are informed of the "Neural Audit" process during ingest.

  (ii) Purpose Limitation: Data collected for identity verification is never repurposed for unauthorized commercial use.

  (iii) Data Minimization: We only process essential biometric and alphabetic "shards" required for the OODA loop.

  (iv) Accuracy: Users have the right to request "Neural Correction" of misidentified data (Section 26).

  (v) Storage Limitation: Temporary residual heatmaps are purged after a successful audit, unless flagged for evidence.

  (vi) Integrity and Confidentiality: All "Neural Fingerprints" are encrypted and vaulted within our Milvus Sovereign Cloud.

2. Handling Sensitive Personal Data (Section 44-47)

- Since UHAKIKI processes Biometric Data (defined as sensitive under the Act), we implement:

  (I) Automated Processing Safeguards: Per Section 35, users are notified when a decision (e.g., a fraud flag) is made solely by the AI, and they retain the right to human intervention.

  (II) Encryption at Rest: Vector embeddings are stored with high-grade encryption to prevent unauthorized disclosure (Section 72).

3. Data Breach Protocol (Section 43)

- In the event of a "Neural Leak," the UHAKIKI system is programmed to:

  (i) Notify the Data Commissioner (ODPC) within 72 hours.

  (ii) Notify the Data Subject in writing without undue delay where there is a real risk of harm.
